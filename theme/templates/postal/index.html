The Evolution of Holocaust in Holland

This display exhibit uses philatelic artifacts and contemporary material to document the destruction of the Jewish population in Holland

learn more

False Hopes and Lasting Thanks: The Battle of Arnhem

Operation Market Garden, in September of 1944, raised hopes of liberation and the end of four years of German occupation. This exhibit looks at the Battle of Arnhem and its aftermath from the perspectives of the Dutch civilians and German and British combatants whose lives intersected for ten days in Arnhem.

learn more

Code Name Yellow: 1940-Invasion and Occupation of Holland

This exhibit and collection will follow the Netherlands during the period of mobilization, especially following the German invasion of Poland in September of 1939, and its experience of the German invasion. German troops crossed the border in the pre-dawn hours of Friday, May 10th, 1940 and by September 15th controlled the country except for a small area of Zeeland, soon to fall.
