    Severed Connection to Arnhem - Returned Mail (continued)

    Following Operation Market Garden postal connections were severed, not only to Arnhem, but also to nearby towns in the Province of Gelderland, such as Velp and Wageningen.

    Wolfheze, a village in Renkum municipality, the site of much activity by 1st Airborne Division, was finally liberated on 16 April 1945.

    On 24 October 1944, there was a roundup (“razzia”) in Bussum (North Holland). Male residents were taken to Arnhem and forced to dig trenches and create defense works for Organisation Todt, the military-engineering company that supplied industry with forced labor and built fortifications such as the Westwall and Atlantik Wall. Through the “Nooddienst” Emergency Service residents of Naarden and Bussum could send parcels and mail to the laborers in Arnhem and vicinity once a week and on the return trip the buses took mail back. Mail usually consisted of an undated postcard, a sign of life, and it received a distinctive red label. Surviving Nooddienst mail is scarce and less than 10 pieces are believed to exist.

    Aftermath

    Cornelia van Limberg Stirum had founded several schools that were located next door to her Nijenburgh residence. On the evening of 17 September, the 3-story school building, known as the Red School, was occupied by 30 men from A and B Troops, 1st Parachute Sq., Royal Engineers, having followed 2nd Battalion along the “Lion” route into Arnhem. They were joined the next day by soldiers of C Company, 3rd Battalion under Major Lewis.

    This combined British force of approximately 60 men set about turning the school into a defensive strong point. The next few days saw ferocious fighting with German troops gradually gaining the upper hand. They fired 51mm mortar rounds directly into the school’s north-facing windows and at about 7 pm on Tuesday, 19 September, a German tank approached to within 30 yards and blew away the northeast corner of the building.

    On Wednesday morning a German tank and self-propelled gun began firing on the schoolhouse. The 45 remaining defenders could do little more than watch as the German guns blew away the roof and top story of the school. Major Lewis and Captain Mackay made preparations to evacuate the burning school, but as the first men began to move, a number were killed and wounded. Major Lewis gave the order to surrender. The Germans surrounded the pitiful survivors and took them prisoner.

    Lady Cornelia, was in poor health and had been moved from her bedroom to the hallway by her maid, Lou Bos, to avoid the constant shelling. The Janssens crossed the line of fire to seek help but were unable to return. Finally, the Germans set fire to Nijenburgh, ignoring pleas from the maid that the Lady would be burnt alive. The maid dragged Lady Cornelia down on a mattress to a safe spot in the garden where she was found the next morning alive but weak, eyes and mouth covered in soot and ash. She died on 23 September 1944 at the age of 76.

    Evacuees

    On 23 September the German military authority ordered the immediate evacuation of the population of Arnhem. Most residents left for the northern and eastern sections of Holland, while approximately 600 chose to stay close to their former homes, taking refuge in the Netherlands Open Air Museum / Nederlands Openluchtmuseum (OLM) which was located just outside the evacuation zone.

    Most evacuees were forced to leave after a round up of men and women eligible for national labor service took place on 3 November, while a small group braved the cold and draughty conditions, staying at the museum until January 1945.

    This letter was sent to Max Vogel, while he was staying at the Open Air Museum, by his wife, who had been evacuated to Heerenveen. The letter is clearly postmarked and it yet does not show any of the usual handstamps indicating non-delivery. Mrs. Vogel writes: “I sent several letters poste restante Velp but please do not try and pick them up… it is already old news by now. I heard from Riek that we too have been plundered. I am distraught, but Max, do not try and go and see for yourself because those bastards shoot just like that, as you know.
